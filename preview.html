🌎 Animal Food Chain Website

A food chain explains how energy moves through nature. Animals need food to survive, and every living thing depends on another part of the ecosystem.

🌱 How The Food Chain Works

☀️

Sun

The Sun provides energy for every living thing on Earth.

➡️
🌿

Plants

Plants use sunlight to make food through photosynthesis.

➡️
🦗

Grasshopper

Grasshoppers eat plants and become food for other animals.

➡️
🐸

Frog

Frogs eat insects like grasshoppers to survive.

➡️
🐍

Snake

Snakes are hunters that eat frogs and rodents.

➡️
🦅

Eagle

Eagles are powerful predators near the top of the food chain.

☀️ Energy Source

Almost all food chains begin with the Sun. Plants capture sunlight and turn it into energy.

🌿 Producers

Producers are living things like plants and algae that make their own food.

🦁 Consumers

Consumers are animals that eat plants or other animals.

🌲 Forest Ecosystem Animals

Rabbit

Primary Consumer

Rabbits eat grass and plants.

📖 Animal History

Rabbits have existed for millions of years and became known for their speed and ability to survive in many environments.

🧠 What They Do

Rabbits spend most of their time searching for plants and staying alert for predators.

⭐ What Makes Them Unique

Their strong back legs help them jump quickly away from danger.

Fox

Secondary Consumer

Foxes hunt rabbits and small animals.

📖 Animal History

Foxes adapted to forests, grasslands, snowy regions, and even cities around the world.

🧠 What They Do

Foxes are clever hunters that quietly stalk prey at night.

⭐ What Makes Them Unique

Their intelligence and hearing make them excellent hunters.

Wolf

Top Predator

Wolves are important hunters in forests.

📖 Animal History

Wolves once lived across huge parts of the world and inspired many ancient legends.

🧠 What They Do

Wolves work together in packs and communicate through howls.

⭐ What Makes Them Unique

Their teamwork makes them one of nature’s greatest hunters.

🛟 Rare Animals That Need Protection

Vaquita 🐬

Critically Endangered

The vaquita is the rarest marine mammal in the world and only lives near Mexico.

⚠️ Biggest Danger

Fishing nets are the biggest threat to its survival.

🌍 How Humans Can Help

Scientists remove dangerous fishing nets and create safer ocean zones.

Amur Leopard 🐆

Critically Endangered

This rare leopard survives in cold forests and has beautiful thick fur.

⚠️ Biggest Danger

Habitat destruction and illegal hunting made their population very small.

🌍 How Humans Can Help

Protected forests and breeding programs are helping them recover.

Red Panda 🐼

Endangered

Red pandas spend much of their lives in trees and use fluffy tails for warmth.

⚠️ Biggest Danger

Forest destruction reduces the bamboo forests they depend on.

🌍 How Humans Can Help

Organizations plant trees and protect bamboo forests.
